Sleeping Beauty



  • Versions of Sleeping Beauty have been published by both Charles Perrault and the Brothers Grimm. Perrault called his heroine Aurora, while the Grimms' referred to their princess as Briar Rose. Disney split the difference and used both names.
  • Prince Phillip was named after another royal that Americans would have been familiar with during that time period.
  • Instead of three fairies, the original version included seven.
  • Mary Costa, the voice of Sleeping Beauty, is a professional opera singer and has performed in more than 40 operatic roles on stage.
