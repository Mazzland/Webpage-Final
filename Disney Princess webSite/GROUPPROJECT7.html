Ariel


  • In the Hans Christian Andersen story that inspired The Little Mermaid, the sea-witch is not a prominent character. She doesn’t even have a name!
  • The blue-green color of Ariel’s fin was specially mixed by the Disney paint lab—they called this new color “Ariel” in her honor.
  • In the opening scene with King Triton and his daughters, you can see Kermit, Mickey Mouse, Goofy, and Donald Duck and in the crowd.
  • In the original development drawings of this film, Ursula was portrayed as a spinefish or a scorpion fish with lots of spikes and spines.
  • Many of the sailors dancing on Prince Eric’s ship are caricatures of the staff who worked on the movie.
  • After being moved to tears by The Little Mermaid, a New Jersey State Trooper called his estranged daughter to repair their relationship. He wrote to Ron Clements (writer/director) to tell him the story.
